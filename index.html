Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

"Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action."

Biography

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. He studied physics and aerospace engineering and joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). Known as the "Missile Man of India," he played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development. He served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007 and remained a beloved figure for his humility, vision, and dedication to youth and education.

Major Achievements

  • Led development of India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III)
  • Key figure in Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998
  • Authored inspirational books like Wings of Fire, Ignited Minds, and India 2020
  • Served as the 11th President of India (2002–2007)
  • Recipient of Bharat Ratna, Padma Bhushan, and Padma Vibhushan
